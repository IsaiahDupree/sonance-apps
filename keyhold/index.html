About

A native macOS password manager for the KeePass database you already use. Keyhold opens and saves standard .kdbx files, so it works alongside KeePass, KeePassXC, and Strongbox — your passwords stay yours, in a file you control, on your Mac.

EVERYTHING RUNS ON YOUR DEVICE
There is no account, no cloud, no network. Nothing you store ever leaves your Mac.

GREAT FOR
• Opening the KeePass vault you already have — no migration, no export
• Unlocking instantly with Touch ID
• Generating strong, unique passwords on the spot
• Keeping logins organized in groups and finding them fast with search

SECURITY
• Argon2id key derivation + AES-256, the same proven KeePass cryptography
• Every file is authenticated (HMAC) — tampering is detected before anything is decrypted
• Touch ID stores a device-bound key, never your master password
• Optional key file, plus auto-lock on idle, sleep, and screen lock
• Copied passwords are concealed and auto-clear from the clipboard

Getting started

  1. Click “Open a KeePass Vault” and choose your .kdbx file.
  2. Enter your master password — and optionally enable Touch ID.
  3. Browse, search, edit, and generate passwords, then Save. Everything stays on your Mac.

Frequently asked questions

Which files does it open?

Standard KeePass .kdbx databases (KDBX 4) — the same files KeePass, KeePassXC, and Strongbox use. Open the vault you already have; there's no migration.

Where is my data stored?

Only on your Mac, in the .kdbx file you choose. There is no account and no cloud — nothing is ever uploaded.

How is it secured?

Argon2id key derivation and AES-256, with HMAC authentication that detects tampering before anything is decrypted. Touch ID stores a device-bound key, never your master password.

What's free and what's Pro?

The full manager is free — open, edit, search, generate, Touch ID, key files, auto-lock. Pro is a one-time purchase adding CSV import, CSV export, and encrypted .kdbx export.
