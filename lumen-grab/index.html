About

Grab text from anywhere on your screen. Press a hotkey, drag a box around any text — a menu, an image, a video frame, a locked PDF, an error dialog — and it lands on your clipboard, ready to paste.

GREAT FOR
• Copying text out of screenshots and images
• Pulling a quote off a paused video or a webinar slide
• Grabbing text from a PDF that won't let you select it
• Copying an error message instead of retyping it

On-device OCR powered by Apple's Vision framework: fast, accurate, and completely private — nothing is ever uploaded.

FEATURES
• Global hotkey — grab without leaving what you're doing
• Accurate on-device text recognition
• Keep the original line breaks or flow into one paragraph
• Grab history + additional languages (Pro)

Free: unlimited grabs to the clipboard. Pro (one-time purchase): history + more languages.

Everything runs on your Mac.

Getting started

  1. Press the global hotkey (Option-Command-G).
  2. Drag a box around the text you want.
  3. The recognized text is now on your clipboard — paste anywhere.

Frequently asked questions

How do I grab text?

Press the global hotkey and drag a box around any text — a menu, image, video frame, or PDF. The recognized text lands on your clipboard.

Is my screen sent anywhere?

No. OCR runs entirely on-device via Apple's Vision framework.

It asked for Screen Recording permission.

macOS requires that permission to read screen pixels. Grant it in System Settings ▸ Privacy & Security ▸ Screen Recording.
