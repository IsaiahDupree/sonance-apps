About

Relay is a client for a Relay hub — a small service you run yourself that aggregates webhooks, RSS/JSON feeds, and HTTP checks into one signal stream. The app connects to the hub you point it at and shows every signal in a live, filterable feed, with an Overview dashboard and a one-tap integrations catalog.

Just want to look around? Turn on Demo mode (offered on first launch, and in Settings → Demo) to explore the whole app with realistic sample data — no hub required.

Getting started

  1. Run a Relay hub, or open the Relay app on your Mac to get a hub URL and pairing code.
  2. On first launch, enter your hub’s URL to create an account, scan the desktop pairing QR, or paste a relay key by hand.
  3. New signals stream into the Feed in real time. Filter by level, mute noisy sources, and mark items seen.
  4. No hub yet? Tap Try the demo to explore every screen with sample data.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need a server to try Relay?

No. Turn on Demo mode to explore the full app with sample data. To receive your own signals you connect the app to a Relay hub you run.

“Create account” showed an error — what happened?

Creating an account reaches out to your hub, so it needs a valid, reachable hub URL. If you don’t have a hub yet, use Demo mode instead — no connection required.

Is my data private?

Yes. Relay has no analytics or tracking, and it talks only to the hub you point it at. See the Privacy Policy.
