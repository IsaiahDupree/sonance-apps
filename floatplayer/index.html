About

FloatPlayer turns any video into a floating window. Import a file, paste a stream link, or pick a sample — then swipe home and it keeps playing in a draggable, resizable Picture-in-Picture window over whatever you're doing.

GREAT FOR
• Following a tutorial while you work in another app
• Keeping a lecture or talk playing while you take notes
• Watching while you message, browse, or read
• Jumping back to the exact second with timestamped bookmarks

FEATURES
• Native Picture in Picture for your videos (Files import, MP4/HLS links)
• Playback speed 0.5×–2×
• Resume where you left off, per video
• Timestamped bookmarks with notes — tap to jump back
• A companion Watch tab for web video

Free. No account. No tracking.

Getting started

  1. On the Player tab, pick a sample, paste a video URL, or Import from Files.
  2. Tap play — then swipe home. The video floats over your other apps.
  3. Drag the floating window anywhere; pinch to resize; tap to pause or return.
  4. Use “Bookmark this moment” to save a timestamp; tap it later to jump back.

Frequently asked questions

Picture in Picture didn’t start — why?

Make sure the video is playing when you leave the app, and that “Start PiP Automatically” is enabled in iOS Settings → General → Picture in Picture.

What formats can I play?

Anything iOS plays natively: MP4/M4V/MOV files and HLS (.m3u8) streams. Import from Files or paste a direct link.

Does PiP work for web videos in the Watch tab?

Web players control their own PiP. Some sites limit floating playback (for example, music content may require a subscription on that site). Your own videos on the Player tab always support PiP.

Is my data private?

Yes. FloatPlayer has no account, no analytics, and collects nothing. Your videos and bookmarks stay on your device.
